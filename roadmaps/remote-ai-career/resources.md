# Remote AI Career Resources

All free. Grouped by what they're for in the [roadmap](roadmap.md).

## Job boards with real remote AI roles
- **[Wellfound](https://wellfound.com/)** — startup jobs with listed salary ranges and remote filters; the best single board for AI startup roles. *(check 2×/week)*
- **[Y Combinator — Work at a Startup](https://www.workatastartup.com/)** — YC companies, many remote-friendly and fast-moving; one profile, many applications.
- **[Hacker News "Who is hiring?"](https://news.ycombinator.com/submitted?id=whoishiring)** — monthly thread (1st of each month); search "REMOTE". Small well-funded startups, often no formal funnel — a good cold email wins here.
- **[We Work Remotely](https://weworkremotely.com/)** — the biggest remote-only board; programming section.
- **[Himalayas](https://himalayas.app/)** — clean remote board with company timezone/location requirements shown up front (saves wasted applications).
- **[Remotive](https://remotive.com/)** — remote-only, decent AI/engineering section.
- **[RemoteOK](https://remoteok.com/)** — high volume; filter aggressively.
- **[ai-jobs.net](https://ai-jobs.net/)** — AI/ML-specific listings incl. remote filters.
- **LinkedIn** — set filters: Remote + posted last week + entry/associate; apply within 48 hrs of posting (application #400 doesn't get read).

## Freelance (Route B)
- **[Upwork](https://www.upwork.com/)** — largest market for the "AI chatbot / document Q&A for small business" niche. One specific offer, 5 bids/week.
- **[Contra](https://contra.com/)** — commission-free freelance platform, growing AI category.
- **[Fiverr](https://www.fiverr.com/)** — productized gigs ("I'll build a RAG chatbot over your docs"); works as inbound while you sleep.
- Later, with experience: [Toptal](https://www.toptal.com/) / [Arc](https://arc.dev/) — screened networks with better rates.

## Talent platforms (Route C accelerators, usually need ~1 yr experience)
- **[Turing](https://www.turing.com/)** — matches Indian/global devs to US companies; vetting tests + steady USD contracts.
- **[Arc](https://arc.dev/)** — remote-only developer matching.
- **[Crossover](https://www.crossover.com/)** — standardized testing, full-time USD remote roles; demanding but transparent pay.

## Salary data (negotiate from facts)
- **[levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/)** — real comp data; check "Remote" and India pages for calibration.
- **[Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/)** — company-level salaries and interview reports (free account).
- Wellfound listings — startups post ranges directly; collect them in your Week-2 spreadsheet.

## Being findable (inbound > outbound over time)
- **[GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com/)** — free portfolio site.
- **[dev.to](https://dev.to/)** / **[Hashnode](https://hashnode.com/)** — free technical blogging with built-in distribution; write up each portfolio project.
- **[Loom](https://www.loom.com/)** — free screen-recording tier for take-home video walkthroughs.
- Open source targets: [MCP servers](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/) (young ecosystem, easy meaningful contributions), [LangChain](https://github.com/langchain-ai/langchain) and friends (high visibility, lots of good-first-issues), [OpenCV](https://github.com/opencv/opencv) (prestigious but C++-heavy and a high review bar — start with issues labeled "good first issue", docs/tutorial fixes, or Python binding tests, and expect slow review cycles).

## Remote work craft
- **[GitLab Handbook](https://handbook.gitlab.com/)** — the canonical all-remote operating manual; read the communication chapter, quote it in interviews.
- **[Deel blog](https://www.deel.com/blog/)** — how global contracting/EOR employment actually works (know what you're signing).
- **[Wise](https://wise.com/)** — the standard for receiving USD payments in India at honest exchange rates (vs. bank wire fees).
